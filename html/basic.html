Historic Places

Groote Kerk

The Grooke Kerk is a Dutch Reformed Church in the Cape Town, South Africa. The church SA’s oldest place Christian worship, built by Herman Schutte in 1841. At first the colonist, landing beginning in 1652 at the Cape of Good Hope, relied on a lay preacher named Willem Wylant.

Castle of Good Hope

The Castle of Good Hope was built in 17th Century in Cape Town, South Africa. In 1936 The Castle was declared a historical monument. Castle was built by the Dutch East India Company. The Casle acted as local headquaters for the South African Army in the Western Cape.

District Six Museum

District Six Museum in the old Methodist Mission Church recalls the life of its once-vibrant racially mixed surbab and its destruction under apartheid. The museum in the Cape Town seek to reconnect with the spirit of the community and provide a different interpretation of the past.
